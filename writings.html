Our First Poem

There is beauty in innocence And innocence in beauty Both are within you And I hope you can see How attractive that can be Don’t confuse ignorance with innocence As I once did Ignorance may be bliss But innocence is the tender kiss Of a father As he says goodnight to his daughter There comes a certain point When a person can see The love that God has for you And me That love is contagious It likes to grow Before awhile it makes you go “Which love do I have for you? And which love do you have for me Which comes first, does it matter? If one before the other Should I stop and see If you could ever have the same love for me?” And if not That’s okay I’ll continue loving you anyway Flowers wilt just as soon as they bloom But I hope the same isn’t true For the friendship between me and you But if it is, That’s okay I wouldn’t have it any other way You see I’ve come to understand That time is fleeting And so are we I once thought that things that end Are best never started For the heartbreak that follows Can be for some too much to swallow But there’s beauty in it all Beginning middle and end If the interest ever arises I would love to take your hand Even if for a moment Just so you know Without a shadow of a doubt That you are important But not because of what I see But because of who God created you to be
